✅ Working a Claim — Three Action Buttons

Each button has a confirm dialog. No action runs until you click Yes.

1✓ Resolve Override
Use when: An Override Rule drove the output (TIMELY_URGENT, DEFERRED, etc.) and you've confirmed the override action is complete.
What it does: Logs resolution to Worked_Claims, marks claim resolved, removes from queue.
💡 A confirm dialog appears first — click Yes to proceed or Cancel to go back.
2📋 Mark as Worked
Use when: You have completed all work on this claim in Epic — filed appeal, routed to coding, adjusted off, etc.
What it does: Permanently logs to Worked_Claims with your email, date, CARC, category, and action. Removes from your queue.
⚠️ Complete the action in Epic FIRST. Mark as Worked is a log — it does not act in Epic automatically. This cannot be undone.
3↩ Release Claim
Use when: You cannot work this claim right now and want to return it to the shared pool.
What it does: Clears your ownership. The claim becomes available for any rep on their next engine run.
💡 Claims auto-release after 10 hours of inactivity regardless — but use this button to release immediately. Supervisors can also release any rep's claim from Settings.
All three buttons show a confirmation dialog before running. Click Cancel to abort, Yes to proceed.

🗺️ Tool Overview — AR Decision Engine vs Note Pre-Processor

Two separate Google Sheets work together. Understand what each one does before you start.

AR_Decision_Engine_Final — Your daily worklist tool
What it is: The main Google Sheet your team works in every day. You export your Epic workqueue, paste it here, run the engine, and work your claims.

When you access it: Every time you work AR — export from Epic, paste, run engine, resolve claims.
Epic_Import tab
Paste your Epic WQ export here. Multiple reps stack their data in this one sheet, each with their own header row.
Claim_Resolver tab
Select an invoice from your dropdown. The engine output (category, root cause, workflow steps) populates automatically.
Claims_Database tab
All processed claim data lives here. The engine writes here after every run. Do not edit manually.
Denial_Lookup tab
The rule library — every CARC/payer combination the engine knows how to handle. New rules from Note Pre-Processor go here.
Override_Rules tab
Rules that override denial lookup — timely filing deadlines, department transfers, allowance variances, etc.
Worked_Claims tab
Permanent log of every claim marked as worked. Date, rep email, action taken.
⚙️ AR Engine menu: Run Decision Engine · Resolve Override · Mark as Worked · Release Claim · Clear All & Reset
Note Pre-Processor — The rule-building tool
What it is: A separate Google Sheet used to create and push new denial rules to the AR Decision Engine. When the engine shows "No Rule Matched" for a claim, you research the denial, write a work note in Google Drive, and use the Note Pre-Processor to turn that note into a live rule.

When you access it: Only when the AR Decision Engine cannot find a rule for a denial scenario. Not part of the daily workflow — you only need it when something is missing.

⭐ = Functions you will use regularly. The others are for maintenance, troubleshooting, or batch operations.
